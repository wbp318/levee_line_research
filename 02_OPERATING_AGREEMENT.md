## ARTICLE IV — MANAGEMENT

**4.1 Manager-Managed.** The Company shall be manager-managed. The day-to-day business and affairs of the Company shall be managed by the Manager.

**4.2 Initial Manager.** The initial Manager is:
```
William Brooks Parker
689 Lensing Lane, Lake Providence, LA 71254
```

**4.3 Powers of the Manager.** The Manager shall have full authority to manage the business and affairs of the Company, including but not limited to:
- (a) Executing contracts, leases, and agreements on behalf of the Company
- (b) Opening and managing bank accounts
- (c) Hiring and terminating employees and contractors
- (d) Applying for and managing grants, loans, and other funding
- (e) Making day-to-day operational decisions
- (f) Purchasing equipment and supplies necessary for research operations
- (g) Filing tax returns and government reports
- (h) Representing the Company in dealings with federal, state, and local agencies

**4.4 Limitations on Manager Authority.** The following actions require approval of Members holding a majority (more than 50%) of total Membership Interests:
- (a) Any single expenditure or commitment exceeding $10,000
- (b) Entering into any loan or debt obligation exceeding $25,000
- (c) Selling or disposing of a substantial portion of Company assets
- (d) Entering into contracts with a term exceeding one year

**4.5 Actions Requiring Supermajority.** The following actions require approval of Members holding at least 66.7% (two-thirds) of total Membership Interests:
- (a) Admission of new Members
- (b) Amendment of this Operating Agreement
- (c) Merger, consolidation, or reorganization of the Company
- (d) Dissolution of the Company
- (e) Sale of all or substantially all Company assets
- (f) Any change to the purpose of the Company
- (g) Any change to the Manager's identity or role

**4.6 Removal of Manager.** The Manager may be removed with or without cause by a vote of Members holding at least 66.7% (two-thirds) of total Membership Interests, excluding the Manager's own interest if the Manager is also a Member.

**4.7 Compensation of Manager.** The Manager shall be entitled to reasonable compensation for services rendered, as determined by the Members. The Manager may also be reimbursed for reasonable expenses incurred on behalf of the Company.

## ARTICLE VI — ALLOCATIONS AND DISTRIBUTIONS

**6.1 Profits and Losses.** Net profits and net losses of the Company shall be allocated to the Members in proportion to their respective Membership Interests (i.e., pro rata).

**6.2 Distributions.** Distributions of available cash shall be made at such times and in such amounts as determined by the Manager, distributed to Members in proportion to their Membership Interests. The Manager shall ensure adequate reserves are maintained for:
- (a) Operating expenses
- (b) Grant compliance obligations and matching requirements
- (c) Tax obligations
- (d) Equipment and research costs

**6.3 Tax Distributions.** To the extent feasible, the Company shall distribute to each Member at least enough cash to cover the estimated income tax liability arising from the Member's share of Company income (based on the highest marginal individual tax rate).

## ARTICLE VII — TAX MATTERS

**7.1 Tax Classification.** The Company shall be classified as a **partnership** for federal income tax purposes. The Company shall file IRS Form 1065 (U.S. Return of Partnership Income) annually.

**7.2 Schedule K-1.** Each Member will receive a Schedule K-1 reflecting their share of Company income, deductions, credits, and other tax items.

**7.3 Tax Matters Partner.** William Brooks Parker shall serve as the Tax Matters Partner (or Partnership Representative under the Bipartisan Budget Act) and shall be authorized to make tax elections and represent the Company before the IRS.

**7.4 Fiscal Year.** The fiscal year of the Company shall be the calendar year (January 1 through December 31).

## ARTICLE VIII — TRANSFER OF INTERESTS

**8.1 Restriction on Transfer.** No Member may sell, assign, transfer, pledge, or otherwise dispose of all or any part of their Membership Interest without the prior written consent of Members holding at least 66.7% (two-thirds) of total Membership Interests.

**8.2 Right of First Refusal.** Before any Member may transfer their interest to a third party, the transferring Member must first offer the interest to the existing Members at the same price and on the same terms. Existing Members shall have 30 days to exercise this right, with the right allocated pro rata among those Members who wish to purchase.

**8.3 Involuntary Transfer.** In the event of a Member's death, disability, bankruptcy, or other involuntary event:
- (a) The Company shall have the first option to purchase the interest at fair market value
- (b) If the Company does not exercise this option within 60 days, the remaining Members may purchase pro rata
- (c) If neither the Company nor the Members exercise the option, the successor in interest may become a Member only with the consent of 66.7% of remaining Membership Interests

---

## ARTICLE IX — MEETINGS AND VOTING

**9.1 Annual Meeting.** The Members shall hold at least one annual meeting. The Manager shall provide at least 14 days written notice.

**9.2 Special Meetings.** Special meetings may be called by the Manager or by Members holding at least 25% of total Membership Interests.

**9.3 Voting.** Each Member shall have voting rights proportional to their Membership Interest. Unless otherwise specified in this Agreement, decisions require a simple majority (more than 50%) of total Membership Interests.

**9.4 Action Without Meeting.** Any action may be taken without a meeting if Members holding the required percentage consent in writing (email is acceptable).

---

## ARTICLE X — BOOKS, RECORDS, AND REPORTING

**10.1 Books and Records.** The Manager shall maintain complete and accurate books and records of the Company at the principal place of business.

**10.2 Access.** Each Member shall have reasonable access to inspect and copy Company books and records during normal business hours.

**10.3 Financial Reporting.** The Manager shall provide to all Members:
- (a) Quarterly financial summaries within 30 days of quarter-end
- (b) Annual financial statements within 90 days of fiscal year-end
- (c) Schedule K-1s by March 15 of each year

**10.4 Grant Reporting.** The Manager shall maintain all records required by federal and state grant programs, including but not limited to expenditure reports, progress reports, and audit documentation. Grant funds shall be tracked separately from other Company funds.

---

## ARTICLE XI — INDEMNIFICATION

**11.1 Indemnification.** The Company shall indemnify and hold harmless the Manager and each Member from any loss, liability, damage, cost, or expense (including reasonable attorney's fees) arising from any act performed or omission made in good faith on behalf of the Company and in a manner reasonably believed to be within the scope of authority granted by this Agreement.

**11.2 Limitation.** No indemnification shall be provided for acts of fraud, willful misconduct, or gross negligence.

---

## ARTICLE XII — DISSOLUTION

**12.1 Events of Dissolution.** The Company shall be dissolved upon the occurrence of any of the following:
- (a) Written consent of Members holding at least 66.7% (two-thirds) of total Membership Interests
- (b) Entry of a decree of judicial dissolution under Louisiana law
- (c) Any event that makes it unlawful for the Company to continue

**12.2 Winding Up.** Upon dissolution, the Manager (or a liquidating agent appointed by the Members) shall wind up the Company's affairs by:
- (a) Completing or terminating all active grants and contracts in accordance with their terms
- (b) Collecting all debts owed to the Company
- (c) Paying or providing for all Company debts and obligations
- (d) Distributing any remaining assets to the Members in proportion to their capital account balances

**12.3 Articles of Dissolution.** Upon completion of winding up, the Manager shall file Articles of Dissolution with the Louisiana Secretary of State.
