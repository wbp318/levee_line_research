# Federal Grant Registration Guide — Levee Line Research LLC

**URGENT: Start this process immediately. Registration takes 2-4 weeks and is required before you can apply for ANY federal grant (SBIR, STTR, USDA, NSF, DOE).**

---

## Overview

Before applying to any federal grant program, you need accounts on three systems:

| System | What It Does | Time to Process |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| SAM.gov | Federal vendor registration (required for all grants) | 7-10 business days (can take up to 4 weeks) |
| UEI Number | Unique Entity Identifier (replaces DUNS) | Issued during SAM.gov registration |
| Grants.gov | Portal where you find and submit grant applications | 1-3 business days after SAM.gov |

**You must complete these in order. SAM.gov first, then Grants.gov.**

---

## Step 1 — Get Your UEI and Register on SAM.gov

**URL:** https://sam.gov/content/entity-registration

### What You Need Before Starting

- [ ] EIN (Employer Identification Number) — apply for this first if you haven't already (see [03_EIN_APPLICATION.md](../formation/03_EIN_APPLICATION.md))
- [ ] Legal business name: **Levee Line Research LLC**
- [ ] Physical address: **689 Lensing Lane, Lake Providence, LA 71254**
- [ ] Bank account info (for electronic funds transfer / direct deposit of grant funds)
- [ ] NAICS code: **541715** (Research and Development in the Physical, Engineering, and Life Sciences)
- [ ] Your SSN (as the responsible party — do NOT store in git)

### Registration Steps

1. **Go to** https://sam.gov and click "Get Started" under Entity Registration
2. **Create a Login.gov account** (or sign in if you have one)
   - Use your business email if possible
   - Set up two-factor authentication (required)
3. **Request a UEI Number**
   - During registration, SAM.gov will automatically assign you a UEI
   - This replaces the old DUNS number system
   - Write down your UEI — you'll need it for Grants.gov and every grant application
4. **Complete the Entity Registration**
   - Entity Type: **Business or Organization**
   - Entity Structure: **Limited Liability Company (LLC)**
   - State of Incorporation: **Louisiana**
   - Physical Address: **689 Lensing Lane, Lake Providence, LA 71254**
   - NAICS Code: **541715**
   - Business Type: Select **Small Business** and **Research Institution** if applicable
5. **Core Data section**
   - Enter your EIN
   - Enter banking information for Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT)
   - This is how the government will pay you if you receive a grant
6. **Representations and Certifications**
   - Complete all required certifications
   - Pay attention to small business size standards
   - For NAICS 541715, the small business size standard is **1,000 employees** — you qualify
7. **Submit and Wait**
   - Processing takes **7-10 business days** (sometimes up to 4 weeks)
   - You'll receive email confirmation when registration is active
   - Check status at https://sam.gov by logging in

### Important Notes

- SAM.gov registration must be **renewed annually** — set a calendar reminder
- Registration is **FREE** — do not pay anyone who claims to register you (common scam)
- If you get stuck, call the Federal Service Desk: **(866) 606-8220** (Mon-Fri, 8am-8pm ET)

## Step 2 — Register on Grants.gov

**URL:** https://www.grants.gov/register

**Prerequisite:** Your SAM.gov registration must be ACTIVE before you can submit on Grants.gov. You can create an account while waiting, but you cannot submit applications until SAM.gov clears.

### Registration Steps

1. **Go to** https://www.grants.gov/register
2. **Create an account**
   - Use the same email as your SAM.gov / Login.gov account
   - You'll need your UEI number (from Step 1)
3. **Link your organization**
   - Organization Name: **Levee Line Research LLC**
   - UEI: [your UEI from SAM.gov]
   - EIN: [your EIN]
4. **Assign roles**
   - You (William Brooks Parker) should be the **Authorized Organization Representative (AOR)**
   - The AOR is the person authorized to submit grant applications on behalf of the LLC
   - You can add additional users later (partners, grant writers, etc.)
5. **Complete the registration**
   - Grants.gov will verify your organization against SAM.gov
   - This takes **1-3 business days**

### Setting Up Grant Alerts

Once registered, set up email alerts for relevant opportunities:

1. Log in to Grants.gov
2. Go to **Search Grants** → **Saved Searches**
3. Create saved searches for:
   - **CFDA 10.212** (USDA SBIR)
   - **CFDA 47.084** (NSF SBIR/STTR)
   - **CFDA 81.049** (DOE SBIR/STTR)
   - Keywords: "artificial intelligence" + "agriculture"
   - Keywords: "precision agriculture"
   - Keywords: "AI" + "farming"
4. Enable email notifications for new postings

## Step 3 — Register with SBA

**URL:** https://www.sba.gov/

### SBA Company Registration

1. **Go to** https://sba.gov/size-self-certification
2. **Create an SBA profile**
   - This certifies you as a small business
   - Required for SBIR/STTR programs
3. **Complete the SBA Company Registry**
   - URL: https://www.sbir.gov/registration
   - This is specifically for SBIR/STTR applicants
   - Enter your UEI, EIN, and business details

### SBA Size Standards

For NAICS 541715, the small business threshold is:
- **1,000 employees or fewer**
- You easily qualify as a startup

## Step 4 — Additional Registrations (Agency-Specific)

### NSF (National Science Foundation)

- **Research.gov:** https://www.research.gov
- Create an account — NSF uses Research.gov for SBIR/STTR Project Pitches
- You'll need this to submit your initial Project Pitch before a full proposal

### USDA NIFA

- Applications go through Grants.gov (already covered above)
- No additional registration needed beyond SAM.gov + Grants.gov

### DOE

- Applications go through SBIR.gov or specific DOE portals
- Register at https://www.sbir.gov if not already done through SBA step
