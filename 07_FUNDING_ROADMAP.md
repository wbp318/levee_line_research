# Funding Roadmap — Levee Line Research LLC

Comprehensive funding strategy for launching and scaling an AI agricultural research company.

**Current Capital:** $500 (Managing Member) + potential partner contributions
**Goal:** Secure enough funding to sustain operations, hire researchers, and build AI ag-tech products

---

## Funding Strategy Overview

```
Phase 1 (Now):        Bridge capital — SBA Microloan + partner contributions ($10K-$50K)
Phase 2 (May-Oct):    First grant — NSF or USDA SBIR Phase I ($175K-$275K)
Phase 3 (Post-award): Stack funding — LA Innovation Retention Grant ($100K) + LA Growth Fund
Phase 4 (Year 2):     Scale — SBIR Phase II ($600K-$1M) + additional grants
Phase 5 (Year 3+):    Sustain — Revenue + larger grants + private investment
```

## Tier 1 — Immediate Bridge Capital (April-May 2026)

### SBA Microloan
- **Amount:** Up to $50,000 (average ~$13,000)
- **Interest:** 8-13%
- **Terms:** Up to 6 years
- **Best for:** Getting the business operational while waiting for grant decisions
- **Apply through:** Nonprofit community-based intermediary lenders
- **Find a lender:** https://www.sba.gov/funding-programs/loans/microloans
- **What to use it for:** Equipment, software subscriptions, cloud computing, initial research costs
- **Pros:** Fast, small amount manageable to repay, builds credit history
- **Cons:** Must repay with interest regardless of business outcome

### Partner Capital Contributions
- Potential from 4 additional members (father + 3 partners)
- Structure as capital contributions documented in Operating Agreement
- Each partner's contribution should be proportional to (or justify) their ownership stake
- Advantages: no interest, no repayment obligation, strengthens the Company's balance sheet

### Personal Funds / Bootstrap
- Current: $500 committed
- Minimize personal debt — focus on non-dilutive grants instead
- Bootstrap period should be as short as possible

## Tier 2 — Federal SBIR/STTR Grants (May-December 2026)

**These are the primary funding targets. Non-dilutive (you keep 100% equity), no repayment required.**

### NSF SBIR Phase I — AI Topics
- **Amount:** ~$275,000
- **Duration:** 6-12 months
- **Process:** Submit a Project Pitch (2-3 pages) → if invited, submit full proposal
- **Best topics for Levee Line Research:**
  - AI2: Computer Vision (crop monitoring, disease detection)
  - AI6: Sustainable AI for Low Resource Environments (rural ag applications)
- **Portal:** https://seedfund.nsf.gov
- **Expected opening:** April-May 2026
- **Strategy:** Submit Project Pitch as soon as portal reopens. NSF likes novel, commercially viable technology.

### USDA SBIR Phase I (via NIFA)
- **Amount:** Up to $175,000 + $6,500 TABA
- **Duration:** 8 months
- **Evaluation:** Scientific merit (40%), Commercial potential (30%), Team qualifications (30%)
- **Relevant topics:**
  - Precision agriculture and variable-rate application
  - Crop monitoring and disease detection via AI
  - Yield prediction models using machine learning
  - Remote sensing and automated scouting
- **Expected solicitation:** May-June 2026
- **Expected deadline:** ~October 2026
- **Portal:** Grants.gov (CFDA 10.212)
- **Strategy:** Apply to both NSF and USDA SBIR — they are separate programs and you can receive both

### DOE SBIR Phase I
- **Amount:** Up to $200,000
- **Duration:** 6-12 months
- **Relevant if:** Your AI research touches bioenergy, renewable agriculture, or energy-efficient computing
- **Genesis Mission:** $293M program, applications due April 28, 2026
- **Portal:** https://science.osti.gov/sbir

### Key SBIR/STTR Requirements
- Must be a US small business (fewer than 500 employees for most; 1,000 for NAICS 541715)
- At least 51% owned by US citizens or permanent residents
- Principal Investigator must be primarily employed by the company
- Must perform the research (not just subcontract it out)
- Register on SAM.gov, Grants.gov, SBA, and SBIR.gov FIRST

## Tier 3 — Louisiana State Programs (Ongoing)

### Louisiana LTTO — Proposal Preparation Assistance
- **Amount:** Up to $5,000
- **Purpose:** Helps write competitive SBIR/STTR proposals
- **Action:** Contact NOW, before you start writing proposals
- **URL:** https://www.opportunitylouisiana.gov/ltto

### Louisiana Innovation Retention Grant
- **Amount:** Up to $100,000
- **Requirement:** Must have received a federal SBIR or STTR award (Phase I or II) after June 15, 2022
- **Disbursement:** Two equal payments over two years
- **Strategy:** Apply immediately after receiving your first SBIR/STTR award
- **URL:** https://www.opportunitylouisiana.gov/news/louisianas-extends-innovation-retention-grant-program

### Louisiana Growth Fund (via SSBCI)
- **Amount:** $250,000-$1,000,000 equity investment
- **Requirement:** 1:1 private capital match
- **Priority:** Very small businesses (<10 employees), SEDI-owned businesses
- **Apply through:** Participating venture fund partners:
  - Boot64 Ventures
  - Innovation Catalyst
  - The Idea Village
  - New Orleans Startup Fund
  - Ochsner Louisiana's Innovation Fund
  - Tulane Innovation Institute
- **Strategy:** Best approached after you have an SBIR award or significant traction

### Louisiana SSBCI Programs (~$113M total)
- **Small Business Loan Guaranty Program** — loan guarantees through participating lenders
- **Collateral Support Program** — if you lack collateral for a traditional loan
- **Loan Participation Program** — direct loan participation
- **Seed Capital Program (LSCP)** — early-stage equity
- **Venture Capital Program (LVCP)** — later-stage equity
- **URL:** https://www.louisianassbci.com/programs/

### Louisiana R&D Tax Credit
- **Credit:** Up to 30% on qualified research expenditures
- **Opens:** July 1, 2026 (new FY2026-2027 allocation)
- **Eligible costs:** Wages for research, supplies, contract research, cloud computing
- **Strategy:** Track all research expenditures from day one. Apply as soon as the new allocation opens.

## Tier 4 — SBA Loans for Growth (When Revenue Supports Repayment)

### SBA 7(a) Loan
- **Amount:** Up to $5,000,000
- **Interest:** 10-13.5% (variable)
- **Terms:** Up to 10 years (working capital/equipment); 25 years (real estate)
- **Down payment:** 10-20%
- **SBA guarantees:** 85% of loans ≤$150K; 75% of loans >$150K
- **Best for:** Scaling operations after initial grant success — hiring, equipment, lab space
- **Apply through:** Any SBA-approved lender
- **Timing:** Best after you have grant revenue or contracts to demonstrate ability to repay

### SBA 504 Loan
- **Amount:** Up to $5,000,000
- **Use:** Fixed assets only (land, buildings, heavy equipment)
- **Structure:** 50% from lender, 40% SBA-backed, 10% your equity
- **Terms:** 10 or 20 years, fixed rate
- **Best for:** If you need to purchase lab space, server equipment, or agricultural research facilities
