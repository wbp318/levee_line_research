# Arkansas Expansion Research — Levee Line Research LLC

Arkansas is viable as a primary formation state or as an operational footprint in parallel with Louisiana. 689 Lensing Lane, Lake Providence sits ~15 miles south of the Chicot County line — Lake Village, AR is the nearest AR county seat. For an AI agriculture R&D company, Arkansas offers genuine strategic advantages that deserve a head-to-head look before the LA Articles of Organization are filed.

This document parallels the existing Louisiana docs (01, 04, 10) for Arkansas and ends with a side-by-side comparison and a recommended decision framework.

---

## Why Arkansas Is on the Table

1. **Geography is irrelevant to domicile.** The farms, drones, sensors, and field-data collection can happen on either side of the river. A Lake Village, AR address and a Lake Providence, LA address are ~15 miles apart and both in the heart of the Mississippi Delta row-crop corridor.
2. **Chicot County is Tier 4 distressed.** Under AEDC's county tier map, Chicot consistently ranks Tier 4 — the most-distressed, highest-incentive tier. This directly unlocks:
   - Advantage Arkansas: **4% payroll credit** on new FTEs, $50K minimum payroll threshold (vs. $125K in Tier 1 counties)
   - Maximum eligibility for the discretionary Create Rebate, ArkPlus, and Targeted Business R&D designations
3. **AR is a targeted-sector state for NAICS 541715.** Research and development in physical/engineering/life sciences is explicitly named as an AEDC targeted sector, which unlocks the **33% Targeted Business R&D tax credit** and the Equity Investment Tax Credit.
4. **Delta Regional Authority covers both sides.** DRA-eligible federal programs (SEDAP, DRA Infrastructure, DRA Workforce) work identically for Chicot (AR) and East Carroll (LA). You don't lose DRA access by siting in AR.
5. **Lower franchise/tax surface.** AR franchise tax on LLCs is a **flat $150/year**. LA's franchise tax is being phased out; for LLCs taxed as partnerships it was already not binding. Roughly a wash on franchise — but AR's individual income tax tops at **3.9%**, comparable to LA's top 4.25%.
6. **Major ag research universities in-state.** UA Division of Agriculture (Fayetteville + Stuttgart Rice Research & Extension Center), UAPB (1890 HBCU land-grant, strong precedent for NSF/USDA "broadening participation" scoring), A-State, UAM (closest to Chicot). All are strong STTR partners.

## Part II — Arkansas Compliance Guide

Parallel to [`04_LOUISIANA_COMPLIANCE.md`](04_LOUISIANA_COMPLIANCE.md) for AR.

### Annual Franchise Tax Report (LLC)

- **Due:** May 1 every year
- **Fee:** $150 flat
- **File at:** https://www.ark.org/sos/franchise/index.php
- **Form:** LLC1_FT
- **Penalty for missing it:** $25 flat + interest; administrative revocation if ignored
- **Set a calendar reminder for April 1** each year

### State Income Tax — Multi-Member LLC (Partnership)

**Partnership return:**
- **Form:** AR1050 (Arkansas Partnership Income Tax Return)
- **Deadline:** April 15 (or 15th day of 4th month after fiscal year-end)
- **Extension:** Federal Form 7004 auto-grants a one-month AR extension; otherwise file Form AR1055-PE
- **Instructions:** https://www.dfa.arkansas.gov/wp-content/uploads/PartnershipTaxInst.pdf

**Individual Arkansas income tax (2026, top bracket 3.9%):**

| Net Income | Rate |
|------------|------|
| $0 – $5,599 | 0% |
| $5,600 – $26,400 | graduated to 3.9% |
| Above $26,400 | 3.9% |

Net income > $94,700: first $4,700 at 2%, remainder at 3.9%. No standard deduction. Social Security not taxed.

### Pass-Through Entity Tax (PET / PTET) — Recommended

- **Rate:** 4.9% ordinary income / 2.45% capital gains
- **Form:** AR1100PET (annual election)
- **Due:** April 15 for calendar-year entities
- **Benefit:** Pays state tax at the entity level, bypassing the federal $10K SALT cap
- **If elected, do NOT also file AR1050**
- **DFA contact:** (501) 682-4775

### Estimated Quarterly Taxes (Individual Members)

- **Form:** AR1000ES (https://www.dfa.arkansas.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026_Final_AR1000ES.pdf)
- **Required if:** expected AR tax liability > $1,000
- **2026 due dates:** April 15, 2026; June 15, 2026; September 15, 2026; January 15, 2027
- **Portal:** ATAP (Arkansas Taxpayer Access Point)
- **Composite entity estimates:** Form AR1000CRES

### Sales Tax

- **Likely not applicable** for a pure research LLC (same as LA analysis)
- **Combined sales tax at Lake Village:** 10.50% (6.5% state + Chicot County local + city)
- **If selling software, hardware, or taxable services later:** apply for a sales tax permit (free) via ATAP
- **Grant income is NOT subject to sales tax**

## Part III — Arkansas Grants & Funding Strategy

Parallel to [`10_LOUISIANA_GRANTS_STRATEGY.md`](10_LOUISIANA_GRANTS_STRATEGY.md) for AR.

### Why Arkansas Grants Matter

AR's SSBCI 2.0 allocation totals **$81.6M** across six programs administered by **ADFA** (Arkansas Development Finance Authority). The AEDC Division of Science & Technology runs a direct **SBIR Matching Grant** program, and NAICS 541715 is an explicitly targeted sector — which unlocks the 33% R&D credit and Equity Investment Tax Credit. Chicot County's Tier 4 status is the highest-benefit tier under Advantage Arkansas.

### 1. AEDC SBIR Matching Grant — AR Analog to LA IRG

- **Amount:** Up to 50% of federal SBIR award — **max $50K Phase I / max $100K Phase II**
- **Authority:** Act 166 of 2017 (§15-3-601)
- **Administered by:** AEDC Division of Science & Technology
- **Eligibility:** AR-based business that has won a federal SBIR award; STTR is not explicitly named in the statute — confirm on case-by-case with AEDC
- **Cadence:** Rolling / discretionary (not entitlement)
- **Apply at:** https://www.arkansasedc.com/science-technology/division/commercialization/sbir-matching-grant
- **Rules PDF:** https://www.arkansasedc.com/docs/default-source/s-t/aedc_sbir-match-33.pdf
- **Contact:** (501) 682-5996

**Comparison to LA IRG:** LA IRG is a statutory $100K on both Phase I and Phase II. AR's cap is lower ($50K) on Phase I but matches ($100K) on Phase II. **LA wins Phase I, parity at Phase II. AR is discretionary; LA IRG is statutory — AR risk is higher.**

### 2. Technology Transfer Assistance Grant (TTAG) — AR Analog to LA LTTO

- **Amount:** Up to $3,750–$5,000 (AEDC pays first $2,500; remainder is 50:50 cost share)
- **Use:** SBIR/STTR proposal prep, consultant fees, database searches, travel
- **Apply at:** https://www.arkansasedc.com/Manufacturing-Solutions/division/consulting-services/technology-transfer-assistance-grant/ttag-guidelines
- **Priority:** Apply at the same time you'd apply for LA LTTO. Both can't be double-dipped for the same proposal costs, but you can pick whichever state matches your formation state.

**Comparison to LA LTTO:** LA offers up to $5,000 (no cost share). AR's $3,750 effective cap with cost share is marginally weaker. **LA wins.**

### 3. AR R&D Tax Credits — Three Tiers

| Credit Program | Rate | Cap | Carryforward | Best For |
|----------------|------|-----|--------------|----------|
| **Targeted Business In-House R&D** | **33%** | $50K/yr × 5 yrs = up to $250K lifetime | 9 yrs | NAICS 541715 fits — DEFAULT for us |
| Strategic Value R&D (with ARA/Div. S&T) | 33% | $50K/yr | 9 yrs | Requires AR strategic-sector designation |
| University-Based R&D | 33% | none stated | 9 yrs | Requires contract with AR university |
| In-House R&D (non-targeted) | 20% | none stated | 9 yrs | Fallback if not granted targeted status |

All AR credits can offset **up to 100% of AR income tax liability** in any year. Targeted Business credit is **transferable/sellable once** — rare feature; if the LLC has low AR tax liability in early years, the credit can be sold to another AR taxpayer for cash.

**Apply BEFORE incurring QREs.** AEDC requires an executed Financial Incentive Agreement before spending counts. This is a hard gate — **contact AEDC Business Finance (501-682-5996) on Day 1** of formation, before any research spend.

**Comparison to LA:** LA R&D credit is 30% for <50 employees, as-of-right (no pre-approval), no cap stated. AR targeted credit is 33% with pre-approval required and $50K/yr cap. **LA wins for ease + flexibility; AR wins on headline rate and lifetime value if pre-approval is secured.** Over 5 years at max QRE spend: AR = $250K, LA = varies but potentially larger (no cap).

### 4. Advantage Arkansas (Payroll Tax Credit)

- **Credit:** **4%** of annual payroll of new FTEs (Tier 4 / Chicot) for 5 years
- **Minimum new payroll:** $50K (Tier 4)
- **Wage floor:** ≥ $16.75/hour avg (2026)
- **Max offset:** 50% of AR income tax liability per year
- **Carryforward:** 9 years
- **Apply through:** AEDC Business Finance

Illustrative: hire 4 employees at $50K each = $200K new payroll × 4% = **$8K/yr × 5 yrs = $40K** over 5 years.

### 5. Create Rebate (Discretionary, Year 3+)

- **Cash rebate** of 3.9%–5.0% of new payroll for up to 10 years
- **Threshold:** $2M new payroll in 24 months (standard) OR $250K new payroll at ≥175% state/county avg wage (technology-based variant — fits an AI R&D firm once it scales)
- Director's discretionary approval required

### 6. Equity Investment Tax Credit (EITC)

- **Rate:** 33 1/3% credit on qualified investment into your LLC
- **State annual cap:** $6.25M across all AR targeted businesses
- **Transferable once** to another AR taxpayer
- **Use case:** Partners or outside investors put capital into the LLC — they (or their assignee) capture a 33% AR tax credit. Material incentive if any investor has AR tax liability.

### 7. ADFA SSBCI 2.0 Programs ($81.6M total)

| Program | Type | Allocation | Best For |
|---------|------|------------|----------|
| Capital Access Program | Capital Access | $621K | Small loans |
| Small Business Revolving Loan Guaranty | Loan Guaranty | $5M | Bank loan gap-fill |
| Bond Guaranty Loan Participation | Loan Participation | $15M | Larger capex |
| DBE Loan Participation / Credit Enhancement | Both | $15M | If qualifying |
| **Arkansas Venture Capital Development Fund** | **Direct equity** | **$26M** | $1M direct investments |
| Arkansas Venture Development Fund | Fund-to-fund | $20M | Investment via partner VCs |

- **Administering entity:** ADFA (not Arkansas Capital Corporation)
- **Contact:** Chuck Cathey, VP Development Finance — charles.cathey@arkansas.gov / (501) 682-5906
- **Master URL:** https://adfa.arkansas.gov/state-small-busines-credit-initiative-ssbci/

**Comparison to LA SSBCI:** LA allocated ~$113M to SSBCI; AR allocated $81.6M. LA has more capital, but AR's direct-equity VC Development Fund is a distinct instrument that can write $1M checks directly into AR-based companies — useful Year 2–3.

### 8. Arkansas Research Alliance (ARA) Impact Grants

- **Total project budget cap:** $75K (ARA pays $60K; institution matches $15K)
- **Eligibility:** LEAD PI must be at an ARA partner university (UAF, UA Division of Ag, UAMS, UALR, UAPB, A-State). **Your LLC cannot be prime.** You participate as a commercial partner on a university-led Impact Grant.
- **Precision Agriculture is an explicit growth area** in the current RFP.
- **URL:** https://aralliance.org/ara-impact-grants/

This is the key mechanism for building a formal relationship with a UA or UAPB PI ahead of an STTR.

### 9. Delta Regional Authority (SEDAP) — Works Either Side

Same as LA analysis — SEDAP covers both sides. LLC is **not directly eligible**; work through Chicot County, City of Lake Village, or **Southeast Arkansas Economic Development District (SEAEDD, Pine Bluff)** as a pass-through.

### 10. ARise (Startup Junkie) + Winrock Science Venture Studio

- **ARise:** Free tech-commercialization coaching, pitch prep, grant-writing. https://arisearkansas.org/
- **Winrock Science Venture Studio:** Specifically serves federal-research-funded startups (SBIR/STTR). Based at Winrock International in Little Rock/Fayetteville.

Both provide what LA LTTO provides — but as ongoing, in-kind services rather than cash. Use alongside TTAG.

### When AR Wins Decisively

- If LLC **attracts outside equity investment** from an AR-resident investor: AR's 33 1/3% transferable EITC is a material incentive (~$33K back on every $100K invested).
- If the STTR partner is **UA Division of Ag, UAPB, or ABI consortium member**: 40% of federal award flows to AR, and the relationship unlocks ARA Impact Grants ($60K/proposal) and strengthens STTR scoring.
- If the LLC sells its **R&D tax credit** to another AR taxpayer: AR's targeted credit is **sellable once** — LA credits are transferable but have a less-developed resale market.
- If the LLC wins **SBIR Phase II**: AR matches LA at $100K, and the combined AR stack (R&D credit + payroll + VC Development Fund eligibility) closes the gap.

### When LA Wins Decisively

- **Formation simplicity** — Parker can self-serve as RA, no commercial RA needed.

- **R&D credit ease** — LA's 30% is as-of-right with no AEDC pre-approval; AR's 33% targeted credit requires an executed Financial Incentive Agreement **before** any QREs are incurred (a procedural trap for first-time applicants).
- **Cash-flow velocity in year 1** — LA stack gets $100K IRG faster than AR's discretionary $50K match.

## Part VI — Recommended Strategic Paths

### Path A: Stay All-In on Louisiana (Default — Current Plan)

Keep the current LA formation plan. Use AR only for field research / sensor deployments on AR farms, without state-level commercial presence. Simplest. Forfeits AR 1890 HBCU STTR angle, AR EITC, and AR VC Development Fund.

### Path B: Pivot to Arkansas Primary

File the AR LLC instead. Optimal if:
1. At least one partner is willing to serve as AR commercial RA or AR resident.
2. An AR university (UA Div. of Ag, UAPB, UAM) is the preferred STTR partner.
3. Outside equity from AR residents is in the near-term plan (activates EITC).
4. Parker is willing to commit to AEDC's pre-approval regimen for targeted R&D credit on Day 1.

### Path C: Form LA, Register Foreign in AR (Most Flexible)

1. File Levee Line Research LLC in Louisiana (as currently planned) — keep the $185 cheap formation, keep Parker as his own RA.
2. Register as a **foreign LLC** in Arkansas (~$300 filing). This gives the company legal standing to operate in AR, open AR bank accounts, sign AR leases, and employ AR residents.
3. Pursue **both** LA and AR grant stacks — most programs require being an in-state business; LA grants flow from LA domicile; AR grants can be claimed if the company has qualifying AR operations (payroll, facilities, QREs incurred in AR).
4. On STTR with a UA or UAPB partner, the AR presence strengthens STTR scoring.

**Path C captures the LA formation stack AND preserves AR-side optionality.** It's the lowest-regret path if the decision matrix is close.

### Path D: Dual Entities (Heaviest, Rarely Worth It)

Form Levee Line Research LLC in LA + form a separately-owned AR entity (e.g., Levee Line Research AR LLC) for AR-specific operations. More overhead, two K-1 chains, two tax returns. Only justified if AR-side activity becomes large enough to warrant separate accounting — not a Day-1 structure.

## Part VII — Decision Framework

**Ask these questions in order. A "yes" to any pushes toward AR (Path B) or hybrid (Path C); a clean "no" keeps Path A:**

1. **Is UA Division of Ag (Fayetteville + Stuttgart RREC) or UAPB the natural STTR research partner for the initial project?**
   If yes → AR has the university, STTR structure already puts 40%+ of award in AR, Path B or C makes sense.

2. **Does the first round of outside investment come from AR-resident investors (AR-based partner or other)?**
   If yes → EITC 33.33% is a meaningful incentive, Path B or C.

3. **Is there a partner willing to serve as AR commercial RA or does the budget absorb ~$125/yr for a service?**
   If no → Path A. The RA barrier is real for a solo-Parker scenario.

4. **Is the LLC willing to commit to AEDC pre-approval on Day 1 (before any research spend) to capture the 33% targeted R&D credit?**
   If no → AR credit drops to 20% default, LA's 30% as-of-right wins, Path A.

5. **Is the first SBIR Phase I target $150K+ such that the $50K AR match (vs $100K LA IRG) is a material delta?**
   If yes → LA IRG advantage matters, Path A (or Path C to keep LA as primary).

3. **Before any R&D spending starts:** Execute AEDC Financial Incentive Agreement for targeted R&D tax credit — this is the hard gate for the 33% rate.
4. **On or before April 28 (DOE Genesis deadline — see [`08_GRANT_CALENDAR.md`](08_GRANT_CALENDAR.md)):** Decide formation state. DOE proposal needs a domicile.
5. **Within 30 days of any federal SBIR award:** Apply for AR SBIR Matching Grant.

## Part IX — What This Doesn't Change

Regardless of formation state:

- **EIN application** (doc 03) is identical federal process
- **Federal registration** (SAM.gov, Grants.gov, SBIR.gov, Research.gov — doc 06) is identical
- **Operating Agreement** (doc 02) structure carries over; swap LA-specific references for AR equivalents
- **Federal partnership return** (Form 1065) is identical
- **NSF, USDA, DOE SBIR/STTR eligibility** is identical
- **Delta Regional Authority access** is identical
- **Grant calendar** (doc 08) federal deadlines are identical

The decision to domicile in AR vs LA is essentially a decision about which state's grant/credit/SSBCI stack to optimize for, and which university ecosystem to anchor to. Both states work. **The most defensible answer is probably Path C (LA primary + AR foreign registration) unless an AR university partnership is already the clear STTR direction, in which case Path B (AR primary) is cleaner.**
