# Bank Account Setup — Levee Line Research LLC

## Required Documents

Bring ALL of the following to open a business bank account:

- [ ] Photo ID (driver's license) for all signatories
- [ ] EIN confirmation letter (CP 575 from IRS)
- [ ] Certified copy of Articles of Organization (from LA Secretary of State, $30)
- [ ] Signed Operating Agreement
- [ ] Initial deposit (amount varies by bank, $25-100 typical)

---

## Multi-Member Considerations

With a multi-member LLC, you need to decide upfront:

### Signatory Authority
Who can sign checks, authorize transfers, and access the account?

**Recommended approach:**
- **William Brooks Parker (Managing Member)** — full signatory authority, can act alone
- **Other Members** — limited or no signatory authority unless specifically granted
- The Operating Agreement (Article IV) gives the Manager authority to manage bank accounts

**Options:**
1. **Manager-only access** (recommended initially) — simplest, prevents unauthorized spending, aligns with manager-managed structure
2. **Dual signature required** above a certain amount — e.g., any withdrawal over $5,000 requires two signatures
3. **All members have access** — not recommended for a 5-member LLC, increases risk

### Resolution to Open Account
Some banks require a **corporate resolution** or written authorization from the LLC to open the account. Prepare a simple resolution:

```
RESOLUTION OF THE MEMBERS OF LEVEE LINE RESEARCH LLC

The undersigned Members of Levee Line Research LLC, holding a majority
of the Membership Interests, hereby authorize William Brooks Parker,
Managing Member, to:

1. Open and maintain one or more bank accounts in the name of the Company
2. Deposit and withdraw funds
3. Execute banking agreements and signature cards
4. Set up electronic funds transfer (EFT) capabilities
5. Obtain a business debit card

Adopted as of [DATE].

William Brooks Parker — Managing Member (51%)
Signature: ____________________________

[Additional member signatures as needed]
```

## Recommended Account Structure

For a research LLC receiving grant funds, set up multiple accounts or sub-accounts:

| Account | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Operating Account** | Day-to-day expenses, member contributions |
| **Grant Funds Account** | Federal/state grant money — keep separate for audit compliance |
| **Tax Reserve Account** | Set aside 30% of income for quarterly estimated taxes |
| **Savings / Emergency** | Business reserve fund |

Mercury and Relay both support multiple sub-accounts at no extra cost — this is a major advantage over traditional banks for grant compliance.

## Grant Fund Management

**Federal grants require strict financial accountability.** Best practices:

1. **Separate accounts** — never mix grant funds with operating or personal funds
2. **Track every dollar** — maintain a ledger showing grant fund receipts and expenditures
3. **Allowable costs only** — grant funds can only be spent on items approved in your grant budget
4. **Retain receipts** — keep documentation for every expenditure, for 7+ years
5. **Time tracking** — if charging salaries to grants, keep detailed time records
6. **Indirect cost rate** — if you don't have a negotiated rate, use the de minimis 10% rate for MTDC (Modified Total Direct Costs)
