# Arkansas Expansion Research — Levee Line Research LLC

Arkansas is now the leading candidate formation state. 689 Lensing Lane, Lake Providence sits ~15 miles south of the Chicot County line — Lake Village, AR is the nearest AR county seat, and the research infrastructure we need (Rohwer Research Station, UAM, UAPB, UA Division of Agriculture) is all north of the river, not south of it. This document is now written under the working assumption that AR is primary; LA remains Path C's "form LA / register foreign in AR" fallback if anything in the AR path becomes unworkable.

---

## Why Arkansas — Updated

1. **Research infrastructure is in Arkansas.** Rohwer Research Station (Desha County, ~30 miles north of Lake Village) is UADA's Delta row-crop research hub — 634 acres, 175 field trials/year, 18,000 plots/year, resident scientist, already instrumented. UAM's new $16.8M **Arkansas Forest Health Research Center** opens Summer 2026 in Monticello (~65 miles NW). UAPB's **AI-for-aquaculture** program is active now. UA Division of Agriculture operates **11 research stations** across the state. There is nothing on the LA side of the river at this density.
2. **NSF EPSCoR / DART.** Arkansas is an NSF EPSCoR state, and the current $20M **DART — Data Analytics that are Robust and Trusted** Track-1 project is a direct thematic match for our stack. LA is also EPSCoR (parity on that alone), but DART's subject matter — robust, interpretable data analytics, with 60+ researchers across 12+ AR institutions — is the exact discipline our STTR partners will pull from.
3. **1890 land-grant access.** UAPB is the only Arkansas institution eligible for **Evans-Allen Research Capacity**, **1890 Capacity Building Grants** ($100K–$750K per award, ~$28M/year program), **1890 Centers of Excellence**, and 1890 Scholarships. LA has no 1890 equivalent. An STTR with UAPB as the non-profit RI stacks on top of these 1890-only federal streams.
4. **Chicot County is Tier 4 distressed.** Under AEDC's county tier map, Chicot consistently ranks Tier 4 — the most-distressed, highest-incentive tier. This unlocks max Advantage Arkansas (4% payroll credit, $50K minimum), Create Rebate, ArkPlus, and Targeted Business R&D designations. See the Advantage AR caveat in Part III — the credit is narrower than it looks for owner-operators.
5. **AR is a targeted-sector state for NAICS 541715.** Research and development in physical/engineering/life sciences is explicitly named as an AEDC targeted sector, which unlocks the **33% Targeted Business R&D tax credit**, the **33⅓% Equity Investment Tax Credit** (transferable once), and **1¼×** weighting on certain other incentive calculations.
6. **Delta Regional Authority covers both sides.** DRA-eligible federal programs (SEDAP, DRA Infrastructure, DRA Workforce) work identically for Chicot (AR) and East Carroll (LA). You don't lose DRA access by siting in AR.
7. **Lower tax surface.** AR franchise tax on LLCs is a **flat $150/year**. AR's individual income tax tops at **3.9%** vs LA's 4.25%. AR's **Pass-Through Entity Tax** (4.9% ordinary / 2.45% capital gains) bypasses the federal $10K SALT cap.
8. **Major ag research universities in-state.** UA Division of Agriculture (Fayetteville + Stuttgart RREC + 11 research stations), UAPB (1890 HBCU land-grant), A-State (Delta Center for Economic Development + ABI node), UAM (closest campus, CFANR, AFRC, new Ph.D., new Forest Health Research Center). All are strong STTR partners; depth and variety are broader than LA.

## ⚠️ Important SBIR/STTR Timing Caveat

**SBIR/STTR programs lapsed at congressional reauthorization on September 30, 2025.** NSF has temporarily paused new Project Pitch submissions. Approximately $4B across 11 agencies is frozen pending reauthorization. This is an industry-wide federal issue, not an AR-specific one.

**Implication for the formation decision:** use the lapse window to (a) form the LLC, (b) get all federal registrations (SAM.gov, Grants.gov, Research.gov, SBIR.gov) active, and (c) line up signed MOUs with university partners. When SBIR/STTR reopens, proposals with existing university partnerships and active federal registrations will go out the door in the first week. Companies that wait to form until SBIR/STTR reopens will be weeks behind.

Current timeline of SBIR/STTR reauthorization is uncertain; watch AEDC's Division of Science & Technology and [sbir.gov](https://www.sbir.gov) for updates.

## Part III — Arkansas Grants & Funding Strategy

Parallel to [`10_LOUISIANA_GRANTS_STRATEGY.md`](../louisiana/10_LOUISIANA_GRANTS_STRATEGY.md) for AR.

**For operational detail — timing, earmarks, reporting burden, clawback triggers, and the pre-commitment trap — see [`13_STATE_GRANTS_TIMING.md`](13_STATE_GRANTS_TIMING.md).** This section summarizes what each program is; doc 13 tells you exactly how and when to engage.

### ⚠️ Read Before Engaging Any AR Incentive

1. **Pre-commitment trap:** For every AEDC credit (R&D, Advantage AR, Create Rebate, ArkPlus, InvestArk), a signed Financial Incentive Agreement (FIA) must be in place **BEFORE** you spend a dollar of qualifying R&D, hire anyone, or sign a lease tied to the project. Spending before the FIA is signed permanently disqualifies those expenses from the credit base.
2. **Targeted Business status is NOT automatic.** It requires $250K equity + 150% wage threshold + sector fit. LLRC at $500 initial capital does not qualify until a partner capital raise or first SBIR award bridges the gap. Until then, **Strategic Value R&D credit (33%, non-transferable) is the fallback**, not the Targeted Business credit.
3. **R&D credits cover wages and fringe ONLY** — not supplies, equipment, cloud compute, or buildings. Federal IRC §41 QRE definitions apply.
4. **State FY runs July 1 – June 30.** EITC's $6.25M annual cap is first-come, first-served; July–September is the safest window.

### 4. ⚠️ Advantage Arkansas — Read This Carefully

Advantage Arkansas is a **state income tax credit** equal to **4% of new FTE payroll** in Tier 4 counties (Chicot qualifies). **It is NOT straightforwardly stackable for owner-operators.** Two walls:

**Wall 1 — Partnership tax law blocks self-hire on W-2.** Under IRS Rev. Rul. 69-184, a partner in a partnership cannot also be a W-2 employee of the same partnership. Any "wages" paid to a partner get recharacterized as guaranteed payments under IRC §707(c) — reported on the K-1, not on a W-2. **So if LLRC remains a partnership for federal tax purposes, William cannot be on payroll, and his compensation does not count for Advantage AR.**

**Workaround:** elect S-corp taxation (Form 2553). Then owner-operators can draw a reasonable W-2 salary + distributions. But S-corp election has non-trivial costs: changes K-1 structure, adds payroll compliance, may conflict with partnership-expected grant reporting. Don't elect S-corp just for Advantage AR — do it only if the full federal SE-tax math favors it.

**Wall 2 — AEDC requires AR taxpayers.** AEDC's rule ([AEDC Job Creation Incentives](https://www.arkansasedc.com/why-arkansas/business-climate/incentives/pages/job-creation-incentives)): *"Employees included in the new additional payroll under the project must be Arkansas taxpayers."* Until William establishes AR residency (see Part VIII), his wages (if any) do not count for Advantage AR even in an S-corp structure.

**What this means practically:**
- **Don't budget Advantage AR around self-hire.** The juice isn't worth the squeeze.
- **Budget Advantage AR around year-2 AR-resident FTE hires** — a data scientist in Pine Bluff ($85K), a field tech in McGehee ($45K), a part-time agronomist in Lake Village. Four AR-resident FTEs at $200K combined payroll = $8K/yr × 5 yrs = $40K credit.
- **Call AEDC Business Finance (501-682-5996) BEFORE any hire** — the Financial Incentive Agreement must be executed before jobs are created, same pre-approval gate as the R&D credit.
- **Credit only has value if AR income tax liability exists to offset.** Early years with losses or grant income sitting in carryforward — 9 years to use it, 50% max/yr.

## Part VIII — Arkansas Residency Playbook (LA → AR move)

Since you're moving to AR, doing this properly affects (a) Advantage AR eligibility, (b) personal income tax filing, (c) LLC RA structure, (d) voting rights in Chicot County, (e) homestead property tax credit.

### Domicile test — three prongs, any one triggers residency

Arkansas uses a three-prong test (2.26 Ark. Code R. 51-102(9)):

1. **Domicile** — physical presence + intent to make AR a permanent home
2. **Maintaining a residence in AR + spending >183 days in AR in the tax year** (rebuttable presumption)
3. Other statutory criteria

For a deliberate move: **domicile is the cleanest path.** Buy or rent a Lake Village residence, move physical belongings, update records.

### AR Individual Income Tax

| Filing status | Form |
|---------------|------|
| Full-year resident | **AR1000F** |
| Part-year resident | **AR1000NR** (same form as nonresident; file as part-year) |

**Part-year filing requirement:** Any part-year resident who received **any gross income while an AR resident** must file, regardless of amount or filing status. (Minimum-income thresholds that apply to full-year residents do NOT apply to part-year residents.)

- Form packet: https://www.dfa.arkansas.gov/office/taxes/income-tax-administration/individual-income-tax/forms/
- AR1000F/AR1000NR FY2025 instructions: https://www.dfa.arkansas.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025_AR1000F_and_AR1000NR_Instructions.pdf
- Filing deadline: follows federal April 15 schedule

### Driver License, Vehicle Registration, Voter Registration

- **Driver license + vehicle registration:** must be obtained **within 30 days** of becoming an AR resident. AR Office of Motor Vehicle / DFA Revenue Office.
- **Voter registration:** where you actually reside. Owning property or a business does NOT establish voting residency. Apply ≥30 days before any election. https://www.sos.arkansas.gov/elections/voter-information/
- Moving-to-AR state service hub: https://portal.arkansas.gov/services-right-for-you/i-am-moving-to-arkansas/

### Homestead Exemption (Chicot County)

- **AR Homestead Property Tax Credit:** up to **$600/year** if the home is the primary residence
- Assessed value of primary residence capped at **5% annual increase** (10% for other properties)
- **Age 65+ or fully-disabled:** taxable value **freezes**
- **Chicot County Assessor:** 108 Main Street, Lake Village, AR — (870) 265-8025 — joedanyeechicotassessor@yahoo.com

### Tie-Breaker Reminders (LA → AR move)

Keep LA ties minimal so LA cannot claim residency in the overlap year:
- Cancel LA voter registration
- Re-title vehicles in AR
- Change driver license
- Open AR primary bank account
- Redirect major mail
- Register any minor children in AR schools
- LA has its own 183-day rule and domicile test (LA R.S. 47:31) — both states will look at intent markers

### Filing year of move

File **AR1000NR (part-year) in AR** AND a **part-year LA IT-540B in LA**. Credit-for-taxes-paid mechanics avoid double taxation on overlap-period income — but both forms must be filed.

### Post-residency benefits

Once AR residency is established:
- Parker can self-serve as LLRC's Registered Agent (saves $125/yr commercial RA fee)
- Parker's wages (if S-corp elected) count as AR payroll for Advantage AR
- Parker gets Homestead Credit
- Parker votes in Chicot County (local political capital for municipal permits, county judge relationships)
