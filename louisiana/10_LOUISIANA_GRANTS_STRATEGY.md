# Louisiana State Grant & Loan Strategy — Levee Line Research LLC

Focused strategy for securing Louisiana state funding for an AI agricultural research company based in East Carroll Parish.

---

## Why Louisiana Grants Matter

Louisiana has **$113+ million** in active state funding programs for small businesses. As an East Carroll Parish company, Levee Line Research qualifies for priority consideration in several programs because:

- East Carroll Parish is classified as **rural and economically distressed**
- Louisiana prioritizes businesses with fewer than 10 employees
- The state has a dedicated AI initiative and innovation brand (LA.IO)
- R&D tax credits of up to 30% are among the most generous in the country
- Multiple programs specifically target SBIR/STTR recipients — stack state money on top of federal grants

## Grant Programs (No Repayment Required)

### 1. Louisiana LTTO — SBIR/STTR Proposal Preparation

**Amount:** Up to $5,000
**Status:** Available NOW, rolling basis
**What it is:** Free money to help you write your first federal SBIR/STTR proposal. The Louisiana Technology Transfer Office pays for professional proposal preparation assistance.

**How to apply:**
1. Contact LED Innovation division: https://www.opportunitylouisiana.gov/ltto
2. Explain you are a Louisiana-based small business preparing an SBIR/STTR application
3. They connect you with resources and reimburse up to $5,000 in proposal prep costs

**Strategy:** Contact LTTO **this week**. Use this money to hire a grant writer or SBIR consultant to help draft your NSF or USDA SBIR Phase I proposal. A professional proposal dramatically increases your odds of winning — first-time SBIR applicants have a ~15% success rate, but with experienced help it jumps significantly.

**Priority: HIGHEST — this is free money that helps you win more money.**

---

### 2. Louisiana Innovation Retention Grant (IRG)

**Amount:** Up to $100,000
**Status:** Available, but requires a federal SBIR/STTR award first
**What it is:** Louisiana gives you up to $100K on top of your federal SBIR/STTR award to keep your business in the state. Disbursed in two equal payments ($50K each) over two years.

**Eligibility:**
- Must be a for-profit, privately owned business based in Louisiana
- Must have received a federal SBIR or STTR Phase I or Phase II award after June 15, 2022
- Must maintain Louisiana operations during the grant period

**How to apply:**
1. Win a federal SBIR or STTR award first
2. Apply through LED: https://www.opportunitylouisiana.gov/news/louisianas-extends-innovation-retention-grant-program
3. Submit proof of federal award, business documentation, and a brief application

**Strategy:** Apply immediately after receiving your first SBIR/STTR award. This is essentially an automatic $100K bonus for winning a federal grant while being based in Louisiana. Factor this into your funding projections — a $275K NSF SBIR Phase I effectively becomes $375K with the IRG stacked on top.

**Priority: HIGH — but dependent on winning a federal award first.**

### 3. Louisiana R&D Tax Credit

**Amount:** Up to 30% of qualified research expenditures
**Status:** New FY2026-2027 allocation opens **July 1, 2026** (current year fully issued)
**What it is:** Dollar-for-dollar tax credit (not a deduction) on money you spend doing research in Louisiana. One of the most generous R&D credits in the country.

**Qualified expenditures include:**
- Wages/salaries for employees performing research
- Supplies and materials used in research
- Contract research payments (65% of amounts paid to outside researchers)
- Cloud computing costs for AI model training and data processing
- Software and hardware used for research
- Drone/sensor equipment for agricultural data collection

**How to apply:**
1. Track ALL research expenditures from day one (start now)
2. After July 1, 2026, apply through LED: https://www.opportunitylouisiana.gov/incentive/research-and-development-tax-credit
3. Submit documentation of qualified research activities and expenditures
4. Credits are issued on a first-come, first-served basis — apply early in July

**Strategy:** Begin a detailed research expenditure log immediately. Every dollar spent on AI development, agricultural data collection, model training, cloud computing, and research-related travel should be categorized and documented. When the allocation opens July 1, submit your application on day one. Credits run out — early applicants win.

**What to track starting today:**
| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| Computing | AWS/GCP charges, API costs (Claude, etc.) |
| Equipment | Drones, sensors, cameras |
| Software | PyTorch, QGIS, development tools |
| Data | Satellite imagery subscriptions, USDA datasets |
| Travel | Farm visits for data collection |
| Contractors | Data scientists, agronomists, consultants |
| Supplies | SD cards, batteries, field equipment |

**Priority: HIGH — start tracking expenses now, apply July 1.**

## Equity Investment Programs (Non-Dilutive to Majority Control)

These programs invest in your company in exchange for equity. You give up a small ownership stake but get significant capital with no repayment obligation.

### 5. Louisiana Growth Fund

**Amount:** $250,000 — $1,000,000
**Status:** Available, rolling applications through participating funds
**What it is:** Part of Louisiana's $50 million SSBCI allocation. Equity investments in high-growth Louisiana startups.

**Requirements:**
- For-profit business based in Louisiana
- High-growth, scalable model
- 1:1 private capital match (you need to raise matching funds from private investors)
- Pre-seed, seed, or Series A stage

**Participating fund partners (apply through these):**
| Fund | Focus | Contact Strategy |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| Boot64 Ventures | Tech startups | Pitch AI ag-tech angle |
| Innovation Catalyst | Early-stage innovation | Emphasize research + commercial potential |
| The Idea Village | New Orleans startup ecosystem | Network through events |
| New Orleans Startup Fund | Gulf South startups | Highlight Delta location, rural impact |
| Ochsner Louisiana's Innovation Fund | Health/life sciences | Frame ag research as life sciences |
| Tulane Innovation Institute | University-adjacent ventures | Propose university partnership |

**Strategy:** Best approached after you have some traction — either an SBIR award, a working prototype, or initial revenue. The 1:1 match requirement means you need to find a private investor willing to put in the same amount. Your partners could count as private investors if they make a qualifying contribution.

**Priority: MEDIUM — pursue after SBIR award or prototype. Start networking with fund partners now.**

## Loan Programs (Must Repay, But Favorable Terms)

### 7. Louisiana SSBCI Loan Programs

Three loan-oriented programs under the $113M SSBCI umbrella:

**Small Business Loan Guaranty Program**
- The state guarantees a portion of your bank loan, making it easier to qualify
- Reduces the bank's risk, so they're more likely to approve you
- Apply through participating lenders

**Collateral Support Program**
- If you don't have enough collateral (property, equipment) for a traditional loan, the state provides additional collateral support
- Useful for a startup with limited hard assets

**Loan Participation Program**
- The state directly participates in your loan alongside a bank
- Reduces the amount any single lender has to put up

**Apply for all three:** https://www.louisianassbci.com/programs/
**Strategy:** Use these if you need a traditional loan but can't qualify on your own. The guaranty program is especially useful — approach a local bank (Citizens Bank, Tensas State Bank) and tell them you're applying through the SSBCI loan guaranty program.

## East Carroll Parish Advantage

Being in East Carroll Parish gives you specific advantages for Louisiana funding:

1. **Rural priority** — multiple programs prioritize rural parishes
2. **Economically distressed** — stronger "broader impacts" narrative for federal AND state grants
3. **Agricultural credibility** — you're in the heart of Delta farming country, not a tech bro from New Orleans talking about farming
4. **Less competition** — most Louisiana startups cluster in New Orleans, Baton Rouge, and Lafayette. East Carroll applications stand out.
5. **SEDI eligibility** — Socially and Economically Disadvantaged Individual programs may apply (check specific criteria)

**Use this in every application.** Your location is a feature, not a limitation. Grant reviewers want to fund companies that bring innovation to underserved areas.
