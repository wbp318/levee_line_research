## What Is This?

Levee Line Research LLC is an AI agriculture research company based in Lake Providence, Louisiana (East Carroll Parish). We build artificial intelligence tools that help farmers in the Mississippi Delta make better decisions — detecting crop diseases earlier, predicting yields more accurately, and optimizing how they apply seeds, fertilizer, and water.

**Why it matters:** Precision agriculture is a $12B+ market growing 12%+ per year. The big players (Bayer, Corteva) charge $50K+/year and target large corporate farms. We target small and mid-size Delta farmers with affordable, research-backed AI tools — a gap nobody is filling.

**Why here, why now:** The federal government is actively funding exactly this through SBIR/STTR grants. Louisiana stacks generous state incentives on top. And we're sitting in the middle of one of the most productive agricultural regions in the world with direct access to the farmers who need this technology.

## What Partners Get

**Ownership:** A percentage of the LLC (out of the 49% non-managing share), proportional to their agreed contribution.

**Profit sharing:** Distributions are pro rata by ownership percentage. The Company will distribute enough to cover each member's tax liability from their K-1 income.

**Voting rights:** Proportional to ownership. Day-to-day decisions are handled by the Manager. Major decisions require majority or supermajority votes (see Operating Agreement for details).

**Limited liability:** Members are not personally liable for company debts or obligations beyond their capital contribution.

**Transparency:** Quarterly financial reports, annual financial statements, and full access to company books and records.

**IP value:** All technology, models, and research data belong to the Company. As the Company grows and the IP becomes valuable, so does each member's ownership stake.

## Why This Works

**1. Almost zero startup cost.** $170 to form. Grants are free money — no repayment, no equity dilution.

**2. Louisiana gives you a bonus for winning federal grants.** The Innovation Retention Grant adds $100K on top of every SBIR award. The R&D tax credit gives back 30% of research spending. These stack.

**3. East Carroll Parish is an advantage, not a limitation.** Grant reviewers want to fund innovation in rural, underserved areas. We're in the heart of Delta farming country with direct access to the farmers who need this technology. That's a stronger pitch than another AI startup in Austin or San Francisco.

**4. The research sells itself.** Crop disease detection, yield prediction, precision application — these are the exact topics NSF and USDA are funding right now. Our NAICS code (541715) qualifies for SBIR programs with a 1,000-employee threshold instead of the usual 500.

**5. Multiple shots on goal.** NSF, USDA, and DOE all run separate SBIR programs. We can apply to all of them. Missing one doesn't kill us.

**6. The IP becomes the asset.** Every model we train, every dataset we build, every algorithm we develop belongs to the Company. That IP has value whether we sell subscriptions to farmers, license to ag companies, or eventually sell the company.

## Key Protections for Members

- **Limited liability** — members are not personally liable beyond their contribution
- **Right of first refusal** — if a member wants to sell, existing members get first dibs
- **Supermajority required** for major changes (adding members, dissolving, selling assets, changing the Manager)
- **Manager can be removed** by 2/3 vote of non-manager members
- **Quarterly financial reporting** required
- **All IP belongs to the Company** — no individual can walk away with the technology
- **Grant funds tracked separately** — full transparency on how federal money is spent
