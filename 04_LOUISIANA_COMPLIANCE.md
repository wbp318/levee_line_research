# Louisiana Compliance Guide — Levee Line Research LLC

Everything you need to stay compliant with Louisiana state requirements as a research LLC.

---

## Annual Report

- **Due:** Every year on the anniversary of your formation date
- **Fee:** $30 (online) or $25 (mail)
- **File at:** https://www.sos.la.gov/BusinessServices/FileBusinessDocuments/Pages/default.aspx
- **What it asks:** Confirm business name, address, registered agent, members/managers, NAICS code
- **CRITICAL:** Missing this filing can result in administrative dissolution of your LLC
- **Set a calendar reminder** for 30 days before the anniversary date

---

## State Income Tax

### Partnership Filing
As a multi-member LLC taxed as a partnership:
- The Company itself does not pay Louisiana income tax
- The Company files a **Louisiana Partnership Return of Income (Form IT-565)**
- Each Member receives a Louisiana equivalent of a K-1
- Each Member reports their share of income on their personal Louisiana return

### Louisiana Income Tax Rates (Individual Members)

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| $0 - $12,500 | 1.85% |
| $12,501 - $50,000 | 3.50% |
| Over $50,000 | 4.25% |

**Note:** Rates are per member based on their share of Company income.

### Filing Deadlines
- Partnership return (IT-565): **April 15** (or 15th day of 4th month after fiscal year-end)
- Can request automatic 6-month extension
- Estimated quarterly payments if expected liability exceeds $1,000

## Self-Employment Tax (Federal)

- **Rate:** 15.3% on net self-employment earnings
  - 12.4% Social Security (on first $168,600 of earnings for 2026)
  - 2.9% Medicare (no cap)
  - 0.9% Additional Medicare Tax on earnings over $200,000
- **Who pays:** The Managing Member and any other Members who are actively involved in the business
- **Passive Members** (those not involved in day-to-day operations) may not owe self-employment tax on their distributive share — consult a tax professional

---

## Estimated Quarterly Tax Payments

If you expect to owe $1,000+ in federal tax for the year, pay estimated taxes quarterly:

| Quarter | Due Date |
|---------|----------|
| Q1 (Jan-Mar) | April 15 |
| Q2 (Apr-May) | June 15 |
| Q3 (Jun-Aug) | September 15 |
| Q4 (Sep-Dec) | January 15 (following year) |

- **Federal:** Use Form 1040-ES
- **Louisiana:** Use Form IT-540ES via LaTAP

## Sales Tax

- **Likely not applicable** for a research LLC that is not selling products to consumers
- If you develop and sell software or products in the future, you may need a Louisiana Sales Tax Certificate
- East Carroll Parish combined sales tax: ~9.95% (4.45% state + ~5.5% parish)
- Apply through LaTAP if needed: https://latap.revenue.louisiana.gov/
- **Grant income is NOT subject to sales tax**

## Occupational License / Business Permit

- Contact **East Carroll Parish Police Jury:** (318) 559-2256
- Address: 400 First Street, Lake Providence, LA 71254
- Ask about occupational license requirements for a research LLC
- Estimated cost: $25-100/year (varies by parish)
- Some parishes do not require one for non-retail businesses — ask

## R&D Tax Credit

Louisiana offers a generous R&D tax credit for qualified research conducted in the state.

- **Credit:** Up to 30% of qualified research expenditures
- **Current Status:** FY2025-2026 credits fully issued. New allocation opens **July 1, 2026**
- **Eligible Expenses:**
  - Employee wages for research activities
  - Supplies used in research
  - Contract research expenses (up to 65% of payments to outside researchers)
  - Cloud computing and AI infrastructure costs (if used for qualified research)
- **How to Apply:** Through Louisiana Economic Development (LED)
- **URL:** https://www.opportunitylouisiana.gov/incentive/research-and-development-tax-credit
- **Strategy:** Begin tracking all qualified research expenditures NOW so you're ready to apply July 1

---

## Franchise Tax

- Louisiana's corporate franchise tax is being **phased out** (repealed effective January 1, 2026)
- LLCs taxed as partnerships were generally not subject to franchise tax
- No action needed

---

## Record Keeping

Keep all records for **7 years**, including:
- Bank statements and canceled checks
- Receipts for all business expenses
- Grant award letters and agreements
- Grant expenditure reports and progress reports
- Member capital contribution records
- Operating Agreement and amendments
- Tax returns (federal and state)
- Contracts with vendors, consultants, and partners
- Payroll records (if/when you have employees)
- Equipment purchase records

**Grant-specific:** Federal grants require meticulous record keeping. Keep grant funds in separate accounts or tracked separately. Auditors may review your books years after a grant closes.
